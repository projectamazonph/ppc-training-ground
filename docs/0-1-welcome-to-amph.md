# Welcome. Your Path to Amazon PPC Work

## What you will be able to do

After this lesson, you can explain what an Amazon PPC virtual assistant does and name the work samples you will build in this course.

## The job in one sentence

An Amazon PPC VA uses account data to make careful advertising decisions, documents the work, and explains the next step clearly.

## Watch first

Watch **What an Amazon PPC VA Actually Does** before you continue. Open the lesson transcript if you prefer to read.

## This is not a memorization course

Amazon PPC has formulas, reports, settings, and acronyms. You will learn them. The real skill is knowing what to do when the numbers change.

For example, a campaign can have high ACoS for different reasons:

- The campaign bought clicks from the wrong searches.
- The product page did not convince shoppers to buy.
- The keyword converts, but the cost per click is too high.
- The campaign is in a planned launch phase and the client accepts a higher cost for now.

The number alone does not tell you which action to take. You need a simple work loop.

## Your work loop

- **Read.** Check the objective and the data.
- **Decide.** Identify the likely problem and safest next step.
- **Change.** Make the approved update in the account or simulator.
- **Explain.** Record what changed, why it changed, and when you will review it.

This loop makes your work reliable. It also makes handovers easier when another VA, manager, or client needs to understand your decision.

You will not leave this course with only a certificate. You will create work samples.

## How to use AMPH

Every skill follows the same pattern:

- Learn the decision.
- Watch a short screen-led explainer.
- Work through a realistic case.
- Practice in a tool or worksheet.
- Explain the decision in client language.
- Check your understanding.

You can repeat a lesson or a tool scenario. Repeating a task after feedback is useful practice, not failure.

## Your first professional habit

Do not guess when information is missing.

Before a PPC task, ask for the facts that control the decision:

- Product or ASIN.
- Objective.
- Budget.
- Target ACoS or profitability rule.
- Current stock position.
- Price, offer, and promotion plans.
- Any approved limits on bids, budgets, or campaign changes.

A good question protects a client account. A fast unsupported change can cost money.

## Client language

Use this sentence when you need information before starting work:

> "Before I build the campaign, please confirm the product goal, daily budget, target ACoS, and whether the listing is in stock and ready to advertise. This will help me set safe bids and measure the result correctly."

## Key takeaway

Your job is not to press buttons quickly. Your job is to make a safe, evidence-based decision and make the decision easy for other people to understand.
