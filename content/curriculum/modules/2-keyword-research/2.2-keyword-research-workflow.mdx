# Keyword Research Workflow & Tools

Knowing what match types are is like knowing how to use a hammer. Knowing **how** to find the right keywords is knowing which nail to hit. This lesson gives you the complete keyword research workflow — the same process Ryan uses to find winning keywords for accounts managing ₱50M+ in ad spend.

> 🎯 **Analogy**: Keyword research is like fishing in a new lake. You could cast randomly and hope for the best (many sellers do this). Or you could study the lake, ask locals what's biting, drop a sonar, and cast where the fish actually are. One approach catches dinner. The other catches weeds.

## Step 1: Research — Cast Your Net Wide

Your goal here is **quantity**. Don't judge yet. Just collect. You need at least 50-100 potential keywords per product to start with.

### Source 1: Amazon Search Term Report

If you have a live campaign, this is your gold mine. Even if it's been running for just 7 days:

1. Go to Campaign Manager → Reports → Create Report
2. Select "Search Term Report" → run for the last 7-30 days
3. Download the CSV

> 💡 **Tip**: If you don't have a live campaign yet, create a small auto-campaign with a $10/day budget and let it run for 2 weeks JUST to generate search term data. It's the best research investment you'll make.

### Source 2: Amazon Brand Analytics (Brand Registry Required)

If you have Brand Registry, Brand Analytics gives you the actual search frequency data for ANY keyword on Amazon — not just your campaigns.

1. Go to Brand Analytics → Amazon Search Terms
2. Enter your product category
3. Export the top search terms with frequency

This shows you **what the market is searching for**, not just what your campaigns triggered.

**Example — "bamboo cutting board" category top searches:**

| Search Term | Search Frequency | Top 3 Products |
|-------------|-----------------|----------------|
| bamboo cutting board | 45,200 | Board A, Board B, Board C |
| cutting board with juice groove | 22,100 | Board X, Board Y, Board Z |
| large cutting board | 18,500 | Board M, Board N, Board O |
| wooden cutting board | 15,800 | Board P, Board Q, Board R |
| cutting board set | 12,300 | Board S, Board T, Board U |

### Source 3: Auto-Campaign Search Term Mining

This is the "free" discovery method. Run an auto-campaign (broad match) and regularly mine the search term report for:

- **New keywords you never thought of** — shoppers tell you what they search for
- **Unexpected long-tail variations** — "bamboo cutting board for meat" might be a winner
- **Misspellings** — If enough people misspell it, it's worth targeting

### Source 4: Amazon Search Suggest

Go to Amazon.com's search bar and start typing your product. Amazon auto-suggests the most searched phrases. These are **real shopper queries** with high volume.

```
Type: "bamboo cutting"
Suggestions:
→ bamboo cutting board
→ bamboo cutting board large
→ bamboo cutting board with juice groove
→ bamboo cutting board for meat
→ bamboo cutting board set
→ bamboo cutting board organic
```

> 💡 **Tip**: Type each letter of the alphabet after your seed keyword (bamboo cutting a, bamboo cutting b, bamboo cutting c...) to surface ALL variations. This is called the **alphabet soup method** and it catches what auto-suggest hides.

## Step 2: Analyze — Filter the Noise

Now you have 200+ potential keywords. Time to separate gold from garbage.

### The Filtering Criteria

| Criterion | Keep | Remove |
|-----------|------|--------|
| **Relevance** | Directly related to your product | Unrelated (e.g., "cutting board accessories" if you sell boards only) |
| **Search Volume** | Monthly searches > 100 | Below 100 — not enough traffic |
| **Intent** | Purchase intent (buy, best, for) | Research intent (how to, what is, vs) |
| **Competition** | Low to medium | Saturated (thousands of advertisers) |
| **Brand Safety** | Generic terms | Branded terms from competitors (legal risk) |

**Real example filtering "bamboo cutting board":**

| Keyword | Keep? | Reason |
|---------|-------|--------|
| bamboo cutting board | ✅ Keep | Perfect relevance, high volume |
| how to clean bamboo cutting board | ❌ Remove | Research intent — unlikely to buy |
| organic bamboo cutting board | ✅ Keep | Strong purchase intent |
| bamboo vs wood cutting board | ❌ Remove | Comparison shopper, unlikely to convert today |
| bamboo cutting board oil | ❌ Remove | Different product (accessory) |
| large bamboo cutting board for meat | ✅ Keep | Specific, high intent, long-tail |

## Step 3: Prioritize — Score Your Keywords

Not all keywords are created equal. Score each one on three dimensions:

### The KRP Score (Keyword Relevance Priority)

| Dimension | Score 1 | Score 3 | Score 5 |
|-----------|---------|---------|---------|
| **Relevance** | Somewhat related | Direct match | Exact product match |
| **Volume** | < 500/mo | 500-5,000/mo | 5,000+/mo |
| **Competition** | High (many ads) | Medium | Low (few ads) |

**Example scoring:**

| Keyword | Relevance | Volume | Competition | Total | Priority |
|---------|-----------|--------|-------------|-------|----------|
| bamboo cutting board | 5 | 5 | 1 | 11 | 🟢 High |
| large cutting board with juice groove | 5 | 3 | 3 | 11 | 🟢 High |
| organic bamboo board | 4 | 1 | 5 | 10 | 🟢 High |
| cutting board for kitchen | 3 | 5 | 1 | 9 | 🟡 Medium |
| bamboo products | 1 | 5 | 1 | 7 | 🔴 Low |

> 💡 **Tip**: Prioritize keywords where **relevance is high** even if volume is low. A low-volume keyword that converts at 15% beats a high-volume keyword that converts at 1%. Every time.

## Step 4: Organize — Build Your Campaign Structure

This step connects keyword research to campaign architecture. You'll organize keywords into themed groups.

```
CAMPAIGN: SP - Bamboo Cutting Boards - Broad
├── Ad Group: Organic & Natural
│   ├── bamboo cutting board (broad)
│   ├── organic cutting board (broad)
│   └── natural wood cutting board (broad)
│
├── Ad Group: Size & Feature
│   ├── large cutting board (broad)
│   ├── cutting board with juice groove (broad)
│   └── extra large cutting board (broad)
│
└── Ad Group: Use Case
    ├── cutting board for meat (broad)
    ├── cutting board for vegetables (broad)
    └── BBQ cutting board (broad)
```

Each ad group has a **single theme**. This lets you write ad copy and set bids that match the search intent.

> 🎯 **Analogy**: Organizing keywords into themed ad groups is like organizing your toolbox. You don't put hammers in the drill drawer. Each tool has its place. When you need a hammer, you know exactly where to reach.

## Putting It All Together: Sample Session

Here's what a real keyword research session looks like for a **bamboo cutting board**:

1. **Research (30 min):** Pull search term report, check Brand Analytics, run auto-campaign mining → Get 180 potential keywords

2. **Analyze (15 min):** Remove irrelevant, non-purchase intent, and branded terms → 95 keywords survive

3. **Prioritize (10 min):** Score by relevance, volume, competition → 32 high-priority, 28 medium, 35 low

4. **Organize (15 min):** Group into themed ad groups → 6 ad groups created

**Result:** A structured campaign launch with 32 high-confidence keywords in 6 tightly themed ad groups. Expected ACoS: 25-35% vs. 45-60% for an unstructured approach.

> 📌 **Key Takeaways:**
> - **Research wide** — Use search term reports, Brand Analytics, auto-campaigns, and Amazon Suggest
> - **Analyze ruthlessly** — Remove non-purchase-intent keywords immediately
> - **Prioritize by relevance** — A relevant low-volume keyword beats an irrelevant high-volume one
> - **Organize by theme** — One theme per ad group for maximum relevance and control
> - **Document everything** — Your keyword research is a living document. Update it weekly.
