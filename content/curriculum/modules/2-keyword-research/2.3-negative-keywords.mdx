# Negative Keywords: The Most Underused Profit Lever

Most sellers spend 80% of their time on **what to target** and 20% on **what to block**. That ratio is backwards. Negative keywords — the terms you tell Amazon NOT to show your ad for — are the single fastest way to improve your ACoS.

> 🎯 **Analogy**: Negative keywords are a "Do Not Sell To" list for your store. You wouldn't let someone walk in, waste your staff's time, and leave without buying — every single day. But that's exactly what happens when you don't use negatives. Shoppers click your ad, you pay for the click, and they bounce because what they searched isn't what you sell.

---

## The Before and After

Let's look at a real example. A cutting board seller runs a broad match campaign for **"cutting board"**:

**Before negatives — $1,000 spend:**

| Search Term | Clicks | Spend | Sales | ACoS |
|-------------|--------|-------|-------|------|
| cutting board | 45 | $30.15 | $74.97 | 40% |
| bamboo cutting board | 38 | $25.46 | $99.96 | 25% |
| *cutting board accessories* | *22* | *$14.74* | *$0* | *100%* |
| *how to clean cutting board* | *18* | *$12.06* | *$0* | *100%* |
| *cutting board oil* | *15* | *$10.05* | *$0* | *100%* |
| *cutting board for kids craft* | *12* | *$8.04* | *$0* | *100%* |
| large cutting board with juice groove | 28 | $18.76 | $49.98 | 38% |
| **Total** | **178** | **$119.26** | **$224.91** | **53%** |

Notice those shaded rows? They're **100% ACoS** — pure waste. $44.89 burned on searches that will NEVER convert.

**After negatives — block the waste:**

| Search Term | Clicks | Spend | Sales | ACoS |
|-------------|--------|-------|-------|------|
| cutting board | 45 | $30.15 | $74.97 | 40% |
| bamboo cutting board | 38 | $25.46 | $99.96 | 25% |
| large cutting board with juice groove | 28 | $18.76 | $49.98 | 38% |
| **Total** | **111** | **$74.37** | **$224.91** | **33%** |

The ACoS dropped from **53% to 33%** — a 20 percentage point improvement — just by blocking 4 types of search terms. Same sales. Less spend.

> 💡 **Tip**: That $44.89 in wasted spend per $1,000? Scale that to an account spending ₱500,000/month and you're burning ₱22,445/month on clicks that will never convert. That's a VA's salary.

## Negative Match Types: Exact vs. Phrase

Just like positive keywords, negatives have match types:

### Negative Exact

Blocks the exact search term only.

```
Negative Exact: "cutting board oil"

Blocks:    cutting board oil
Does NOT block: cutting board oil for wood
               organic cutting board oil
```

Use negative exact when you want to block a specific term but leave room for variations.

### Negative Phrase

Blocks any search that contains that phrase in that order.

```
Negative Phrase: "cutting board oil"

Blocks:    cutting board oil
           cutting board oil for wood
           organic cutting board oil
           best cutting board oil
Does NOT block: oil for cutting board (wrong word order)
```

Use negative phrase when you want to block an ENTIRE category of searches.

> 🎯 **Analogy**: Negative exact is like telling your doorman "don't let in anyone named John Smith." Negative phrase is like saying "don't let in anyone whose name has John in it."

## The Three Sources of Negative Keywords

### Source 1: Your Search Term Report (Every Week)

This is your primary negative keyword source. Run the search term report weekly and look for:

| Category | What to Look For | Action |
|----------|-----------------|--------|
| **High Spend, No Sales** | Spent > $10 with 0 sales | Add as negative phrase |
| **Wrong Product** | Search for a different product than yours | Add as negative phrase |
| **Research Intent** | "how to," "what is," "tutorial," "review" | Add as negative phrase |
| **Competitor Brands** | Other brand names you can't compete with | Add as negative exact |
| **Free/Cheap** | "free," "cheap," "used," "refurbished" | Add as negative phrase |

### Source 2: Amazon Search Query Performance Report

Go to Brand Analytics → Search Query Performance. This shows you which searches are NOT resulting in purchases for your brand or category. If the conversion rate for a search term in your category is 0%, add it as negative.

### Source 3: Common Sense (Don't Skip This)

Before you even launch a campaign, think about what terms are obviously wrong:

**For a "bamboo cutting board" campaign, add these negatives pre-launch:**

| Negative | Why |
|----------|-----|
| cutting board oil | Different product category |
| cutting board accessories | Different product category |
| how to | Research intent |
| what is the best | Comparison shopping |
| free | Low-quality traffic |
| cheap | Low ACoS, high return rate |
| used | Pre-owned, not your market |
| replacement | Parts/accessories buyer |
| DIY | Makes their own — won't buy |
| paper | Different material entirely |

> 💡 **Tip**: Add at least 20-30 negative keywords BEFORE launching a campaign. This is called **pre-emptive negation** and it saves you from burning money on day one.

## The Negative Keyword Hierarchy

Not all negatives go in the same place:

```
CAMPAIGN-LEVEL NEGATIVES (apply to all ad groups)
├── Generic waste: free, cheap, used, how to
├── Irrelevant categories: oil, accessories, parts
├── Competitor brands: (branded terms you can't use)
└── Wrong intent: tutorial, review, vs

AD GROUP-LEVEL NEGATIVES (apply to specific ad groups)
├── Ad Group "Organic & Natural"
│   └── Negatives: large, extra large, with handles
├── Ad Group "Size & Feature"
│   └── Negatives: organic, bamboo (already in other group)
└── Ad Group "Use Case"
    └── Negatives: small, mini, decorative
```

**The rule:** If a negative applies to ALL your ad groups (like "free" or "how to"), put it at campaign level. If it only applies to one theme (like "organic" not belonging in the "Size" group), put it at ad group level.

## How Often to Update Negatives

| Phase | Frequency | Time Required |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| **First 2 weeks** | Every 3 days | 10 min |
| **Weeks 2-6** | Weekly | 10 min |
| **Mature campaign** | Bi-weekly | 5 min |
| **Stale/unchanged** | Monthly | 5 min |

The first two weeks are critical. This is when new search terms flood in and you need to block the junk before it accumulates.

> 📌 **Key Takeaways:**
> - Negative keywords are your **fastest ACoS improvement lever** — often drops 10-20% in one session
> - Add **pre-emptive negatives** before launching — block the obvious waste from day one
> - Run the search term report **weekly** — look for high-spend, no-sales terms
> - Use **negative phrase** for broad categories, **negative exact** for specific terms
> - Put **campaign-level** negatives where they apply everywhere, **ad group-level** for theme-specific blocks
> - A well-maintained negative list grows over time and keeps your campaigns efficient as they scale
