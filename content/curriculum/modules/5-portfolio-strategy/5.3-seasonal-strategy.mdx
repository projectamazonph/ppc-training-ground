# Seasonal Strategy & Promo Planning

Amazon's traffic doesn't flow evenly throughout the year. There are massive spikes — Q4, Prime Day, Valentine's Day, Back to School — and quiet valleys. The difference between a good PPC manager and a great one is how they handle these swings.

> 🎯 **Analogy**: Seasonal advertising is like farming. You don't plant, water, and harvest at the same pace all year. There's a **planting season** (preparation), a **growing season** (execution), and a **harvest season** (reaping the rewards). Trying to harvest in planting season gets you nothing. Trying to plant in harvest season means you miss the crop.

---

## The Amazon Seasonal Calendar

| Season | Months | Traffic | ACoS Pattern | Action |
|--------|--------|---------|-------------|--------|
| **Q1 Lull** | Jan-Mar | 🟢 Low | High — conversion drops | Trim budgets, consolidate campaigns |
| **Spring** | Apr-May | 🟡 Rising | Medium — improving | Test new keywords, prepare summer products |
| **Prime Day** | Jul | 🔴 Peak | Low (if prepared) | Aggressive budgets, new launch timing |
| **Q3 Prep** | Aug-Sep | 🟡 Medium | Medium | Build inventory, stock up for Q4 |
| **Q4 Peak** | Oct-Dec | 🔴 Peak | High (competition drives CPC up) | Highest budgets, extend ad schedules |
| **Post-Q4** | Late Dec | 🟢 Low | Very high | Burn off inventory, analyze data |

## The Three-Phase Seasonal Strategy

Every seasonal event needs three phases:

### Phase 1: Pre-Season (4-8 weeks before)

**Goal:** Build awareness, test keywords, collect data.

| Action | Timeline | Budget |
|--------|----------|--------|
| Research seasonal keywords | 8 weeks before | Normal |
| Launch auto-campaigns for discovery | 6 weeks before | +20% |
| Test ad copy with seasonal messaging | 5 weeks before | Normal |
| Build landing pages / A+ Content for seasonal | 4 weeks before | Normal |
| Ramp up budgets | 3 weeks before | +30-50% |

**For Q4 (starting in August):**
```
August:     Research "Christmas gift" keywords, launch auto-campaigns
September:  Test ad copy, increase budgets 20%
October 1:  Full seasonal budgets active, all campaigns live
October 15: Optimize based on first 2 weeks of data
```

> 🎯 **Analogy**: Pre-season is like planting seeds. You don't see results immediately. But if you don't plant in August, you have nothing to harvest in November.

### Phase 2: In-Season (The Event Period)

**Goal:** Maximize conversions, capture traffic, protect profitability.

| Action | Frequency | Budget |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Monitor ACoS daily | Daily | Max budget |
| Add negatives aggressively | Every 2 days | Protects from junk traffic |
| Increase bids on top performers | Every 3 days | Captures peak intent |
| Check inventory levels | Daily | Prevents stockouts |
| Pause low performers | Every 3 days | Redirects budget to winners |

**For Q4 (October-December):**
```
October:   Monitor, optimize, add seasonal keywords
November:  Peak budgets, daily optimization, watch inventory
December:  First 2 weeks = max spend, Last 2 weeks = taper off
```

> 💡 **Tip**: During Q4, check your campaigns EVERY DAY. This is not the time for a "set and forget" approach. Competition increases, CPCs rise, and a campaign that was profitable in September can be bleeding money in November if you're not watching.

### Phase 3: Post-Season (1-4 weeks after)

**Goal:** Harvest learnings, consolidate campaigns, prepare for next season.

| Action | Timeline | Budget |
|--------|----------|--------|
| Reduce budgets to normal levels | Day 1 after | -50% |
| Run end-of-season search term report | Day 2 | Normal |
| Harvest winning seasonal keywords | Day 3 | — |
| Add seasonal negatives | Day 3 | — |
| Save seasonal campaign as template | Week 2 | — |
| Document learnings for next year | Week 3 | — |

**For Q4 (January):**
```
Week 1: Slash budgets, pull final data, add Q4 terms as negatives
Week 2: Analyze best and worst performing campaigns
Week 3: Build Q4 2027 campaign template while learnings are fresh
Week 4: Final report. Archive campaigns. Rest.
```

> 💡 **Tip**: Save your successful seasonal campaign structure as a **template** you can duplicate next year. Include ad groups, keywords, negatives, and bid strategies. Next year you'll launch in 30 minutes instead of rebuilding from scratch.

## Seasonal ACoS Expectations

Understand that seasonal ACoS is NOT the same as normal ACoS:

| Period | Typical ACoS | Competitive Pressure | Strategy |
|--------|-------------|---------------------|----------|
| **Normal (Jan-Sep)** | 20-30% | Low-Medium | Optimize for profit |
| **Prime Day** | 25-40% | High | Accept higher ACoS for volume |
| **Q4 Peak (Nov)** | 30-50% | Very High | Accept highest ACoS of the year |
| **Post-Q4 (Jan)** | 35-60% | Low | Reduce bids, accept lower volume |

**The Q4 trade-off:**
Your ACoS will go up in Q4. That's expected and acceptable — if your total profit also goes up.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                 SEASONAL PROFIT CALCULATION                   │
├──────────────┬───────────────┬───────────────┬───────────────┤
│              │  Normal Month │   Q4 Month    │  Difference   │
├──────────────┼───────────────┼───────────────┼───────────────┤
│ Ad Spend     │   ₱50,000     │   ₱150,000    │  +₱100,000    │
│ ACoS         │   25%         │   40%         │  +15%         │
│ Sales        │   ₱200,000    │   ₱375,000    │  +₱175,000    │
│ Gross Profit │   ₱50,000     │   ₱93,750     │  +₱43,750     │
└──────────────┴───────────────┴───────────────┴───────────────┘
```

Even though ACoS went from 25% to 40%, **total profit increased by ₱43,750** because sales volume more than made up for the efficiency drop.

> 💡 **Tip**: Most advertisers PANIC when ACoS climbs in Q4 and cut budgets — exactly when they should be spending MORE. As long as unit profitability is positive, higher ACoS during peak season is better than lower ACoS at lower volume. Don't optimize for ACoS. Optimize for profit.

## Pre-Built Seasonal Campaign Structure

Here's a template you can duplicate for any seasonal event:

```
📁 PORTFOLIO: 🎄 Q4 2026 Holiday
│
├── CAMPAIGN: SP - Holiday Gift Keywords - Broad
│   └── Ad Group: Gift Keywords
│       ├── Christmas gift for mom (broad)
│       ├── holiday present for wife (broad)
│       └── stocking stuffer ideas (broad)
│
├── CAMPAIGN: SP - Holiday Gift Keywords - Exact
│   └── Ad Group: High-Intent Gifts
│       ├── [bamboo cutting board gift] (exact)
│       └── [gift for home cook] (exact)
│
├── CAMPAIGN: SP - Seasonal Boost - Existing Products
│   └── Ad Group: Core Products (increased bids)
│       └── Existing best performers with season keywords added
│
└── CAMPAIGN: SB - Holiday Brand Awareness
    └── [Seasonal creative with gift messaging]
```

## Key Seasonal Dates for Amazon Sellers (US Market)

| Event | Best Time to Start Preparing | Peak Ad Spend Window |
|-------|------------------------------|---------------------|
| **Valentine's Day** | Mid-January | Feb 1-14 |
| **St. Patrick's Day** | Late February | Mar 1-17 |
| **Easter** | Late February | Last 2 weeks before Easter |
| **Mother's Day** | Mid-April | Last 3 weeks before |
| **Father's Day** | Late May | Last 2 weeks before |
| **Prime Day** | 6 weeks before | The 2-day event + 1 week before |
| **Back to School** | Mid-July | Aug 1 - Sep 15 |
| **Halloween** | Early September | Oct 1-31 |
| **Black Friday/Cyber Monday** | September | Nov 1 - Dec 1 |
| **Christmas** | **August** | Nov 15 - Dec 20 |
| **Post-Christmas Sales** | December 26 | Dec 26 - Jan 5 |

> 🎯 **Analogy**: If you're not preparing for Christmas in August, you're waiting until the party has already started to buy decorations. By the time you're ready, all the good spots are taken and you're paying premium prices for whatever's left.

## The Seasonal PPC Calendar Template

Create your own version of this for each season:

```
SEASON: [Name]
DATES: [Start] → [Peak] → [End]
BUDGET: [Normal] → [Peak] → [Post]

PRE-SEASON CHECKLIST:
[ ] Research seasonal keywords (date)
[ ] Create seasonal campaign structure (date)
[ ] Test seasonal ad copy (date)
[ ] Set up portfolio budget (date)
[ ] Check inventory levels (date)

IN-SEASON CHECKLIST:
[ ] Daily monitoring (all dates)
[ ] Weekly negative keyword review (dates)
[ ] Adjust bids based on performance (dates)
[ ] Check competitor activity (dates)

POST-SEASON CHECKLIST:
[ ] Reduce budgets to normal (date)
[ ] Run final search term report (date)
[ ] Save as template for next year (date)
[ ] Document learnings (date)
```

> 📌 **Key Takeaways:**
> - Seasonal PPC follows **three phases**: Pre-Season (plant), In-Season (harvest), Post-Season (recover)
> - **Start Q4 preparation in August** — not October. The early bird captures the traffic.
> - **High ACoS during peak season is acceptable** if total profit increases — optimize for profit, not ratio
> - Use the **seasonal calendar** to plan your budget increases at least 6-8 weeks before each event
> - **Save seasonal campaigns as templates** — next year you can duplicate and launch in 30 minutes
> - The biggest mistake: cutting budget when ACoS rises during peak season. Don't panic — ride the wave.
