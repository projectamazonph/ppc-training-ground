## Intent

Screen-led explainer teaching search-term report triage as five clear actions: keep (performing within objective), harvest (move a proven term to a controlled exact target), reduce (relevant but too expensive), negate (irrelevant or fails the evidence rule), and watch (not enough data yet). Walks through two worked cases — a relevant high-CVR/high-ACoS term that should NOT be negated but re-bid, and an irrelevant low-CTR term that should be negated — then shows an action log with evidence and next-review-date, before sending learners into the STR simulator. Confident, calm, professional; no talking head; keyword-vs-search-term distinction, five action cards, two case studies, and the action log carry the lesson. Source script: docs/VIDEO-EXPLAINER-SCRIPTS.md, script 7 "Search-Term Triage: Keep, Harvest, Reduce, Negate, Watch" (Module 7, Search Term Triage, before 7.3 STR Triage Prep).

## Notes

- Fully silent build: no narration audio, no music. Narration text from the source script is burned in as on-screen captions instead of TTS, since the script's own per-scene timestamps are already authoritative and more precise than anything a TTS-timing pass would produce.
- Production rules from docs/VIDEO-EXPLAINER-SCRIPTS.md apply: one decision per screen, high contrast, large numbers, no tiny screenshots, one clear closing action.
- This script's five action labels (Keep, Harvest, Reduce, Negate, Watch) are used verbatim as given, even though the repo's actual 7.3 lesson currently teaches a slightly different taxonomy (Keep/Harvest to Exact/Decrease Bid/Add Negative Keyword/Increase Bid). Reconciling the two is a separate future task, not addressed by this video.
- Part of an 8-video pack; script source is docs/VIDEO-EXPLAINER-SCRIPTS.md.
