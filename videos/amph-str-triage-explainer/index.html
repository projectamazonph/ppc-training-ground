Open the STR Scenario

A keyword is what you target.

A search term is what the shopper typed.

The search-term report tells you what your campaign actually bought.

You have five useful actions.

Keep means the term is performing within the objective.

Harvest means move a proven search term into a more controlled target, usually exact.

Reduce means the term is relevant but too expensive.

Negate means the term is irrelevant or fails your evidence rule.

Watch means you do not have enough data yet.

Here is a common mistake.

A term converts well but has high ACoS because CPC is too high.

Do not call it irrelevant.

Calculate the maximum CPC and lower the bid or placement pressure.

A converter may become useful at a lower price.

Now look at an irrelevant query.

It receives impressions, little engagement, and spend without a sale.

This is a negative candidate.

Choose negative exact when you want precision.

Choose negative phrase only when you are certain that blocking the phrase will not remove useful traffic.

Record the action, evidence, and next review date.
