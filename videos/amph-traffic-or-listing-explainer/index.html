Amazon advertising has a simple path.

A shopper sees the ad, clicks, visits the product page, and may order.

Your job is to find where the path breaks.

If impressions are high but click-through rate is weak,

start with traffic relevance and click appeal.

Are we targeting the right query? Does the main image make the product easy to understand?

Is the price competitive? Is the item in stock and winning the featured offer?

If people click but do not order, look at the detail page.

Check the product promise, images, price, reviews, variation choice, shipping,

and whether the landing product truly matches the search.

A relevant keyword should not be blamed for a weak product page.

Use this rule.

Low CTR points you toward targeting and search-result appeal.

Low conversion points you toward the listing and product fit.

High conversion with high cost points you toward bid and placement math.

The signals can overlap, so write your evidence before you change anything.
