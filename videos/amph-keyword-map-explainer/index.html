A keyword list is research.

A keyword map is a plan.

Do not paste every term into one campaign and hope the algorithm sorts it out.

For every target, record four things.

The term or ASIN. The shopper intent. The match type or product target. The action you expect to take.

A proven high-intent term may belong in exact. A discovery term may belong in broad or auto.

An irrelevant phrase belongs in the negative plan.

Think in routes.

Discover terms with auto and broad. Validate terms with phrase or controlled product targeting.

Scale proven terms with exact. Exclude irrelevant traffic with negative targeting.

The route helps you decide where a target lives and how you will measure it.

Keep branded terms separate from non-branded research and performance work.

A branded shopper has a different intent and often a different efficiency profile.

Separate campaigns make reporting and budget decisions clearer.
