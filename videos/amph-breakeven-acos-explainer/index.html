Open the Max-CPC Worksheet

Before you judge ACoS, know the economics.

This product sells for thirty dollars.

Product cost, shipping, Amazon fees, and other direct costs total twenty-one dollars.

Nine dollars remain before advertising.

Break-even ACoS is the highest advertising cost you can carry

before ad-attributed sales stop producing contribution profit.

Here, nine divided by thirty equals thirty percent.

At thirty percent ACoS, advertising is covering itself.

It is not creating extra profit.

Your client may choose a lower operating target

depending on growth, overhead, and business goal.

Next, calculate a maximum CPC.

Use selling price multiplied by target ACoS multiplied by conversion rate.

If the client uses a twenty-five percent ACoS target

and the keyword converts at ten percent,

the maximum CPC is thirty times point two five times point one.

That is seventy-five cents.

If market clicks cost one dollar twenty,

that does not mean you force the bid to one dollar twenty.

The math says the keyword cannot meet the target

at that conversion rate.

Your options are to bid below the market,

improve conversion, accept a different objective,

or find less expensive traffic.

Maximum CPC is not permanent.

It changes when price, target ACoS, or conversion rate changes.

Record your assumptions beside every calculation.

A client or manager should be able to see

why you picked the bid and when you need to recalculate it.

Now complete two calculations in the worksheet.

Write one sentence for each:

"Our maximum CPC is ___ because ___."

This is the sentence you will use

when someone asks why you did not simply bid higher.
