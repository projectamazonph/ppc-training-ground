A number is not a decision.

ACoS at 40% does not automatically mean lower the bid.

First, you need context.

Ask four questions in this order.

What is the campaign trying to do?

Do we have enough data?

Is the traffic relevant?

Can the listing convert the traffic we send?

The same ACoS can mean very different things

for a launch campaign, a mature exact campaign,

and a product with a weak listing.

Example one.

The campaign has high impressions but very few clicks.

This is usually a traffic-match or click-appeal problem.

Check the search terms, the target, main image,

price, and featured offer.

Lowering a bid might save money,

but it does not solve why shoppers ignore the ad.

Example two.

The campaign receives clicks but few orders.

This is often a conversion problem.

Check stock, price, reviews, images, and product fit.

Do not punish a relevant keyword

before you know if the listing is the real constraint.

Example three.

The keyword converts, but CPC is too high for the target.

Now bidding is the lever.

Calculate the maximum CPC you can afford,

then reduce the bid or placement adjustment.

A converting term may still be valuable.

Expensive does not always mean irrelevant.
